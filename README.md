## 💡 Interview Tips

- **Research the company's tech stack before the interview.** Look for job postings, LinkedIn, and engineering blogs to spot the tools they use.
- **Use the STAR method** (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for every behavioral question — even technical ones benefit from structure.
- **Pause before answering.** It's better to think for 5 seconds than to ramble for 2 minutes.
- **Quantify everything you can.** "I reduced ticket volume" is weak. "I reduced ticket volume by 47% through automation" is memorable.
- **Ask clarifying questions in scenario rounds.** It shows critical thinking and mirrors how real IT problems work.
- **Don't fake knowledge.** If you don't know something, say: "I haven't worked with that directly, but here's how I'd approach learning it..."
- **Prepare 3 strong stories** — one about solving a hard technical problem, one about cross-functional collaboration, one about a failure and what you learned.

## 🎤 Interview Styles

### Technical Screen
Usually a 30–45 min call with a hiring manager or senior engineer. Expect a mix of conceptual questions and "how would you handle X" scenarios. No whiteboard, but be ready to talk through your process clearly.

### Panel Interview
Multiple interviewers at once — often IT, HR, and a business stakeholder. Each person evaluates a different dimension. Stay consistent across your answers.

### Take-Home / Practical Assessment
Some companies send a scenario to respond to in writing or a lab exercise. Focus on structure, documentation quality, and your reasoning — not just the answer.

### Behavioral Round
All STAR-format questions. These are just as important as technical rounds for IT leadership roles. Prepare stories around: conflict resolution, prioritization under pressure, leading change, and mentoring others.

## 🖥️ IT Fundamentals

**Q: What is the difference between RAM and storage?**
> 💡 Think about what's temporary vs. persistent. How does each affect performance?

**Q: What happens when you type a URL into a browser and hit Enter?**
> 💡 This is a classic. Walk through the full journey — DNS, TCP/IP, HTTP request, server response, rendering. Each step matters.

**Q: What is the difference between 32-bit and 64-bit operating systems?**
> 💡 Think about memory addressing and application compatibility.

**Q: A user's computer won't turn on. Walk me through your troubleshooting process.**
> 💡 This tests your methodology. Are you systematic or random? Start from the most obvious (is it plugged in?) and work outward.

**Q: What is the difference between a driver and firmware?**
> 💡 One lives on the OS side, one lives closer to hardware. How does each get updated?

**Q: What are the differences between HDD, SSD, and NVMe storage?**
> 💡 Speed, form factor, and use case. When would you recommend each?

**Q: What is the purpose of a BIOS/UEFI?**
> 💡 What runs before the OS loads? Why does that matter for IT support?

**Q: Explain the difference between a process and a thread.**
> 💡 Relevant for troubleshooting performance issues and understanding Task Manager.
